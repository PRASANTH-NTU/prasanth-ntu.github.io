Supervised Learning - Logisitic Regression

What we will learn here

Linear models are a class of models that are widely used and extensively studied in the last few dacades, with roots going back over a hundred years. Linear Models can be used for both Classification & Regression problems.

Logisitic Regression is similar to linear regression, but instead predicting the a continuous value, it predicts which class/ category the input data belongs to.

We will go over the intuition of the algorithm (skip the theory for now, I might add in the future), apply it to both synthetic & real-world dataset to see exactly how it works, and gain an intrinsic understanding of its inner-workings by writing code from scratch & tuning its most important parameter alpha (regularization strength) to see its effect on model complexity and generalization. Finally, we will explore the imporant parameters of the model, its strength & weakness.
